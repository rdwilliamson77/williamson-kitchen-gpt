--- layout: recipe title: hicken Stock permalink: /recipes/chicken-stock.html --- # hicken Stock ## Ingredients - 5 lb chicken bones (backs, wings, necks, and/or carcasses) - 1 large onion, quartered (skin on for color) - 2 carrots, chopped - 2 celery stalks, chopped - 1 head garlic, halved crosswise - 1 small bunch parsley stems - 1 bay leaf - 1 tsp black peppercorns, whole - 1 sprig thyme - 1 gallon cold water (plus more as needed) - {'optional': '1 tbsp tomato paste (for richer color and umami)'} - {'optional': '1 leek (cleaned and chopped)'} ## Instructions 1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Spread chicken bones on a sheet pan and roast for 45 minutes until well browned, turning once. 2. In a large stockpot, combine roasted bones, cold water, and any browned bits from the roasting pan. 3. Slowly bring to a gentle simmer over medium heat. Skim off any foam or scum that rises. 4. Once simmering, add vegetables, herbs, and aromatics. Do not boil; maintain a gentle bubble. 5. Simmer uncovered for 6–8 hours, replenishing water as needed to keep bones submerged. 6. In the final hour, taste the stock for body. Add more time for richness, if needed. 7. Strain through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth into a clean container. Discard solids. 8. Cool quickly in an ice bath. Store in fridge up to 5 days or freeze in portions up to 6 months. ## Notes - * - * - T - o - - M - a - k - e - - C - h - i - c - k - e - n - - B - a - s - e - - ( - R - e - d - u - c - e - d - - S -
